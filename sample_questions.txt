======================================================
  20 CHALLENGING SCENARIO-BASED STUDENT QUESTIONS
  (Real problems — practical solutions from policies.txt)
======================================================

1.  I'm a junior and I just found out I'm 3 credits short of the graduation
    requirement. I only have senior year left. What should I do to make sure
    I still graduate on time?

2.  I failed Algebra I as a freshman and my GPA dropped to 1.8. Now I'm in
    10th grade and I want to go to a 4-year university. Is it already too
    late for me, or is there a realistic path forward?

3.  My family's income is too high to qualify for the Pell Grant but way too
    low to actually pay for college. What financial aid options do I have
    that aren't just loans?

4.  I took the SAT twice and got 1150 both times, but my ACT practice score
    is around 26. The college I want has a 1300 SAT average. Should I keep
    retaking the SAT, switch to the ACT, or apply test-optional?

5.  I'm a senior and I got into my dream school through Early Decision, but
    my family just had a financial emergency and we can't afford the cost.
    Can I back out of an Early Decision acceptance?

6.  I have ADHD and I struggle badly during timed exams. My school has never
    offered me any accommodations. What can I officially request and how do
    I go about getting it?

7.  I want to apply to three highly selective colleges but I can't afford the
    $75 application fee for each one. What are my options?

8.  I'm a first-generation college student and neither of my parents went to
    college, so they can't help me with any of this. I don't know where to
    start — what is the most important thing I should do first in 11th grade
    to prepare for college?

9.  I've been accepted to both a 4-year university at $48,000/year and a
    local community college at $3,500/year. I want to become a software
    engineer. Which path makes more financial sense and what does the
    community college transfer route actually look like?

10. I'm being bullied at school and it has moved to social media. I reported
    it to a teacher but nothing happened. What are my legal rights and what
    formal steps can I take to force the school to act?

11. I'm in 11th grade with a 3.9 GPA and I want to stand out on college
    applications. I currently take two AP classes. Should I load up on more
    APs next year, or are there other ways to strengthen my application?

12. I scored a 4 on my AP Calculus BC exam but the university I got into says
    they only accept a 5 for credit. I don't want to retake Calculus in
    college. Are there other ways to get credit for what I already know?

13. My school doesn't offer IB or AP classes at all. Will that hurt my
    chances of getting into a competitive college when other applicants
    have those courses on their transcript?

14. I'm a senior and I missed 14 days of school this semester because of a
    serious illness. I'm worried about truancy and losing credits. What
    documentation do I need and what are my rights in this situation?

15. I want to be an electrician and everyone keeps telling me I have to go
    to college. I hate the idea of $40,000 in debt. What are my actual
    options after high school to reach that career goal without a 4-year
    degree?

16. I took the PSAT in 11th grade and scored in the 96th percentile. Someone
    told me this could get me a full scholarship. How exactly does the
    National Merit process work and what do I need to do next?

17. I have a 3.2 GPA, solid extracurriculars, and strong recommendation
    letters, but my SAT score is only 1080. The school I want is
    test-optional. Should I submit my score or leave it out, and how do
    I make the rest of my application as strong as possible?

18. I got rejected from all five colleges I applied to in Regular Decision.
    It's April and I don't know what to do. What realistic options do I
    still have at this point in the year?

19. I'm overwhelmed — I have 5 AP classes, a part-time job, and I captain
    the soccer team. I haven't been sleeping and I feel like I'm falling
    apart. What resources does my school have to help me, and what should
    I practically do right now?

20. My parents are undocumented and are afraid to fill out the FAFSA because
    it asks for a Social Security Number. I was born in the US so I'm a
    citizen. Am I still eligible for federal financial aid, and how do I
    fill out the FAFSA when my parents don't have an SSN?
